A significant security lapse at Anthropic has inadvertently revealed the existence of the company's most powerful AI model to date — internally named **"Mythos."**

## What Happened

Nearly 3,000 unpublished assets were exposed through an unsecured content management system that was accessible without any login credentials. The breach was discovered by cybersecurity researchers and reported by Fortune.

The leaked documents describe Mythos as representing a **"step change"** in AI capabilities, with significantly improved performance in:

- **Reasoning** — deeper logical inference and problem-solving
- **Coding** — substantially better software engineering capabilities
- **Cybersecurity** — enhanced ability to identify and address vulnerabilities

## The Irony

Perhaps most notably, the breach highlights a growing irony in the AI industry: the same AI coding tools that Anthropic builds — like Claude Code — can now automate the discovery of exposed assets like these. The tools that make companies more productive also make them more vulnerable.

## Anthropic's Response

Anthropic attributed the issue to "human error in CMS configuration" and emphasized that the leak was "unrelated to Claude, Cowork, or any Anthropic AI tools." The company also noted these were "early drafts of content considered for publication."

## What This Means

The leak of Mythos comes at a critical time for the AI industry, with every major player racing to develop more capable models. If Mythos delivers on its promise of a "step change" in capabilities, it could reshape the competitive landscape significantly.

The question now: can Anthropic maintain its security posture while pushing the boundaries of AI capability?
