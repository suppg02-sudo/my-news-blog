As part of a deal announced in January, Apple now has **"complete access"** to Google's Gemini AI model in its data centers — and is using it to train smaller, on-device "student" models.

## The Partnership

Apple is leveraging **knowledge distillation**, a technique where a large, powerful "teacher" model (Gemini) trains smaller, more efficient "student" models that can run locally on iPhones, iPads, and Macs.

These distilled models:
- Require significantly less computing power
- Can operate entirely on-device
- Are specially tuned for Apple's hardware
- Preserve user privacy by keeping data local

## The Strategy

This represents a fundamental shift in Apple's AI approach. Rather than building everything from scratch or relying entirely on cloud-based AI, Apple is:

1. **Leveraging Google's massive investment** in Gemini's training
2. **Creating efficient local models** optimized for Apple Silicon
3. **Maintaining the privacy narrative** that Apple is known for

## Competitive Implications

The deal puts Apple in a unique position — using a competitor's best AI to build its own differentiated experience. It's a pragmatic move that acknowledges:

- Training frontier models from scratch costs billions
- On-device AI is the future for privacy-sensitive applications
- Apple's strength is hardware integration, not raw AI research

## What's Next

Expect to see these distilled models power the next generation of Siri, along with on-device features across Apple's ecosystem. The hybrid cloud-on-device approach could become the industry standard.

The age of **AI everywhere, running locally** is closer than ever.
